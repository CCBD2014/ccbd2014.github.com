The future trend of Big Data


Panel Chair and Moderator: Songlin Hu（Chinese Academy of Sciences）
Panelists: Bin Wang (Chinese Academy of Sciences), Charles Zhang (Hong Kong University of Science and Technology), Fangming Liu (Huazhong University of Science and Technology), Lin Gu (Hong Kong University of Science and Technology)

Abstract: The availability of enormous data triggers the rapid growth of big data processing on top of large-scale distributed systems. Researchers from both the industry and academia worked together to develop various kinds of technologies and systems. Almost every part of the computer system, such as file systems, data management systems, parallel computing engines and in-memory system technologies, faces intensive changing. Numerous concepts and models are proposed, accompanied by hundreds of new systems. Without a mature theoretical foundation, it is often difficult to identify the future trend of Bid Data in such a foggy cloud. In this panel, panelists from various areas of information retrieval and data mining, database system, networking, software engineering and Big Data application will discuss the state-of-the-art, challenges and the trend of big data. We would not attempt to draw a conclusion, but we expect to outline a vision from the fusion of ideas from different disciplines.

Bio: Lin Gu is an Assistant Professor at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST). He received B.S. from Fudan University, M.S. from Peking University, and Ph.D. from the University of Virginia. He developed the Virtk (a.k.a. t-kernel) operating system, the CCMR cloud platform, and the DVM datacenter-scale virtual machine technology. He currently leads the development of the Data Thinker system, which is likely the fastest big data technology today. Before joining HKUST, he worked at the Founder's Group, Microsoft and Google, and participated in the development of several large-scale systems including Yahoo Ad servers in China and Gmail's global backend system. He has published more than forty research papers at conference and journals like INOFORCOMM, ICDCS, IEEE TOC, IEEE/ACM TON, ACM TOSN, IEEE JSAC etc., and received many Best Paper Awards from conference like ACM Sensys and RTAS. He participated in organizational and referee services in more than fifty academic conferences and journals.
